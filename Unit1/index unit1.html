Mothman

Known for stealing catalytic converters, Mothman has been the center of local legends for nearly 60 years. Each year, Mothman devotees from around the world gather in Point Pleasant, West Virginia to pay hommage to this benevolent cryptid. Past sightings have been interpreted as an ominous warning of disaster to come by the locals. Many tourists hope to catch a glimpse of the mysterious creature, but few are fortunate enough to bear witness.

Noteable Mothman Sightings*:
  1. November 12th, 1966: a group of grave diggers in Clendenin claimed they saw a man like figure flying low through the trees near a cemetery
  2. November 15th, 1966: two young couples reported to police that they spotted a large, grey, flyingn man with red eyes near the TNT north area north of Point Pleasant.
  3. December 15th, 1967: the Silver Bridge crossing the Ohio River in Point Pleasant suffered a sudden collapse, resulting in the death of 46 people.

    Following the collapse, there were no more reported Mothman sightings in the area.

Sheepsquatch

Though not as widely known outside of the Point Pleasant, West Virginia area, sightings of the Sheepsquatch date back to the mid 1990s. Initially the creature was said to run from humans, but the legend of the Sheepsquatch has become more aggressive over time. There is speculation that this wooly, white, ram horned creature hails from the TNT area which is best known for Mothman sightings.
